The Game

What is it?a game we built ourselves.

Dodge balls fly. Power-ups are chosen. One team comes out on top — if the chaos doesn’t get you first." — Deadly Dodge Ball, 2026
01 Overview

Deadly Dodgeball is a fast-paced multiplayer team game built around unpredictable ball mechanics and round-based competition. Players face off in teams where positioning, timing, and quick reactions decide the outcome of each round.


The game features Augments offered after each round, constantly shifting gameplay and preventing repetitive strategies.


Each match is designed to feel chaotic yet controlled, where teamwork and adaptability matter more than individual survival.

02 Features
Real-time multiplayer synchronisation via Roblox networking
Augments System — Boost your character with special augments.
Custom-built 3D map modelled from scratch
Custom VFX — trails, impacts, power-up auras
sound design — SFX, ambient layers, soundtrack
03 Discipline Split

The project was divided cleanly across three disciplines with zero overlap — Environment Art, VFX & Sound Design, and Game Development. Each person owned their domain entirely.


As animation students building a functioning video game, every technical challenge we solved was a new skill acquired. The result is a complete, deployable product.

04 Platform & Tools
Roblox Studio — primary game engine
Lua — core gameplay systems, player interactions, round logic
Autodesk Maya — 3D modelling
Roblox Studio — VFX & Effects

Development

How we built it.five phases, zero shortcuts.

01
Phase 01 — Concept & Design
Defining the Game

Alignment sessions to lock the concept, core loop, and art direction before a single asset was made. Classic dodgeball — then we asked: how do we make it deadly?

02
Phase 02 — Environment Blockout
Building the World

Grey-box blockout of the map to define spatial flow and cover placements.

03
Phase 03 — Core Game Logic
Making it Play

Ball throwing, collision, elimination, round flow — all wired via Lua.

04
Phase 04 — VFX, Sound & Polish
Adding the Feel

Particle effects, hit impacts, kill-streak auras layered over a stable game. Final textures, lighting, and a complete audio suite transformed prototype into product.

05
Phase 05 — Final Pass & Ship
Final Pass

External testers ran multiplayer sessions while Faizan handled final balancing and testing. Augments refined, edge cases fixed, UI polished — one strong, complete map shipped clean.

Obstacles

What got hard.how we got through it.

01
Learning Curve

Working with Roblox Studio involved a learning curve while understanding its tools and workflow.

Resolved
02
Arena Design

Designing the arena wasn’t straightforward. Figuring out what to add, what to remove, and how it affects gameplay took time.

Resolved
03
Augments

Augments are introduced at the start of each round, adding variation and unpredictability. Each ability was iterated through multiple test sessions to ensure no single power dominated matches.

Resolved
04
Sounds & Music

Sound effects and music were sourced from copyright-free libraries and carefully selected to match the game's pace and intensity. Finding the right assets took time but helped create a more engaging experience.

Resolved
05
Collaborative Workflow

The project was developed collaboratively with responsibilities divided across design, gameplay logic, and visual elements. Regular testing and feedback helped refine both mechanics and overall player experience.

Resolved
06
Player Interaction

Designing how players interact with each other and the ball took time to get right for smooth gameplay.

Resolved
Takeaways

What stuck.lessons we won't forget.

01
playability first ✦
Feel Before Finish

A game that feels good beats one that looks perfect but isn't fun. Mechanics were validated before aesthetics every single time.

02
Tune the Game ✦
Balancing Gameplay

Balancing the gameplay required testing different scenarios and adjusting mechanics to keep matches fair and enjoyable.

03
just ship it ✦
Ship and Iterate

Every rough version taught us something the previous one didn't. Waiting for perfect before sharing is a trap every creator falls into once.

04
juice matters ✦
VFX is Feel, Not Decor

Adding particles and sound to a mechanic transformed how it felt — not just how it looked. Game juice is a design tool.

05
Built with practice ✦
Initial Learning

Roblox Studio required time to understand its tools and workflow. Getting comfortable with the environment was a gradual process that improved with practice.

06
fresh eyes ✦
External Testing is Essential

Players outside the team saw issues invisible to us. Fresh eyes are the most valuable and underused debugging tool in any project.
