The Game

What is it?a game we built ourselves.

"Dodge balls fly. Power-ups drop. One team comes out on top — if the chaos doesn’t get you first." — Deadly Dodge Ball, 2026
01 Overview

Deadly Dodgeball is a fast-paced multiplayer team game built around unpredictable ball mechanics and round-based competition. Players face off in teams where positioning, timing, and quick reactions decide the outcome of each round.


The game features dynamic powerup that are introduced after each round, constantly shifting the flow of gameplay and preventing repetitive strategies.


Each match is designed to feel chaotic yet controlled, where teamwork and adaptability matter more than individual survival.

02 Features
01
Real-time multiplayer synchronisation via Roblox networking
02
Collectible power-ups — speed, explosive throws, shields
03
Custom-built 3D map modelled and textured from scratch
04
Hand-crafted VFX — trails, impacts, power-up auras
05
Original sound design — SFX, ambient layers, soundtrack
03 Discipline Split

The project was divided cleanly across three disciplines with zero overlap — Environment Art, VFX & Sound Design, and Game Development. Each person owned their domain entirely.


As animation students building a functioning video game, every technical challenge we solved was a new skill acquired. The result is a complete, deployable product.

Brainstorming GDD Art Direction

Phase 02 — Environment Blockout
Building the World

Grey-box blockout of the map to define spatial flow and cover placements. Developer tested physics on it immediately — structure first, aesthetics second.

Grey-Box Spatial Design Iteration
03
Phase 03 — Core Game Logic
Making it Play

Ball throwing, collision, elimination, round flow — all wired via Lua. Multiplayer sync was the hardest challenge: server-client architecture had to be airtight.

Lua RemoteEvents Physics
04
Phase 04 — VFX, Sound & Polish
Adding the Feel

Particle effects, hit impacts, power-up auras layered over a stable game. Final textures, lighting, and a complete audio suite transformed prototype into product.

External testers ran multiplayer sessions while Faizan handled final balancing and testing, ensuring stable gameplay. Power-ups were refined, edge cases fixed, and UI polished—focusing on one strong, complete map and shipping it clean.

Obstacles

What got hard.how we got through it.

01
🌐
Multiplayer Sync

Roblox automatically handles multiplayer synchronization and server management, which allowed us to focus more on gameplay design, testing, and balancing rather than backend complexity.

Resolved
02
🎨
Arena Design

Each arena is designed to influence movement, positioning, and player interaction. Layout decisions affect how players dodge, take cover, and control the flow of the game during each round.

Resolved
03
⚖️
Power-Up Balance

Powerups are introduced at the start of each round, adding variation and unpredictability to every match.Powerups were iterated and adjusted through multiple test sessions to ensure no single ability dominated the gameplay. The goal was to keep matches fair and add variety to the gameplay.

Resolved
04
🔊
Sound Without a Studio

Sound effects and music were sourced from copyright-free libraries and carefully selected to match the game’s pace and intensity. Finding the right assets took time, but helped in creating a more engaging gameplay experience.

Resolved
05
🤝
Collaborative Workflow

The project was developed collaboratively, with responsibilities divided across design, gameplay logic, and visual elements. Regular testing and feedback helped refine both the mechanics and overall player experience.

Resolved
06
📏
Scope Control

The focus was kept on delivering one polished gameplay experience rather than expanding into multiple unfinished ideas.

Resolved
Takeaways

What stuck.lessons we won't forget.

01
playability first ✦
Feel Before Finish

A game that feels good beats one that looks perfect but isn't fun. Mechanics were validated before aesthetics every single time.

02
talk more ✦
Communication is Technical

Bridging art, audio, and code requires a shared vocabulary. Miscommunication is a bug. Clarity became a team discipline.

03
just ship it ✦
Ship and Iterate

Every rough version taught us something the previous one didn't. Waiting for perfect before sharing is a trap every creator falls into once.

04
juice matters ✦
VFX is Feel, Not Decor

Adding particles and sound to a mechanic transformed how it felt — not just how it looked. Game juice is a design tool.

05
limits = ideas ✦
Constraints Breed Creativity

Roblox's engine limits forced solutions we wouldn't have otherwise found. Every constraint is a creative prompt in disguise.

06
fresh eyes ✦
External Testing is Essential

Players outside the team saw issues invisible to us. Fresh eyes are the most valuable and underused debugging tool in any project.
